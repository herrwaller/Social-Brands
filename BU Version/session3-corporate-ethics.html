第一节

Morality as a Productive Resource

道德作为生产性资源

The shortcomings of pure order ethics are not only a defensive justification for an independent corporate ethics — the Brent Spar case suggests a more assertive argument: morality, or the lack of it, always has economic consequences. Attending to moral concerns may involve higher short-term costs, but it also proves to be an economically sound strategy in the long term (Noll 2013: 134).

纯秩序伦理的缺陷不仅是独立企业伦理的防御性理由——布伦特斯帕案例提示了一个更积极的论点：道德，或其缺失，始终产生经济后果。关注道德问题在短期内可能涉及更高成本，但从长远来看也被证明是一种经济上合理的策略（Noll 2013: 134）。

Why Moral Standards Pay Off
道德标准为何有回报
  • Consistent adherence to moral standards builds trust
  • 坚持道德标准能够建立信任
  • Reduces transaction costs when concluding contracts with partners
  • 降低交易成本——在与合作伙伴签订合同时
  • Opens new opportunities for cooperation
  • 开辟新的合作机会
  • Facilitates internal and external communication
  • 促进内外部沟通
Entrepreneurial Responsibility
企业责任
  • Ethics is an investment in future business success — in a sustainably successful business policy (Homann 1999: 10)
  • 伦理是对未来商业成功的投资——实现可持续成功的商业政策（Homann 1999: 10）
  • Responsibility extends beyond value creation — shared responsibility for the operation of value creation processes
  • 责任超越价值创造——对价值创造过程运营的共同责任
  • Through voluntary self-commitment and participation in regulatory reforms
  • 通过自愿自我承诺和参与监管改革

Ethical companies are not only acting in a competitive disadvantage — they can gain competitive advantages through trust and by taking into account the values of consumers.

cf. Noll 2013: 135

Companies primarily acquire social responsibility by generating profits — and with them not only advantages for the company, but additional benefits for the various stakeholders and society. However, morally responsible companies are only afforded this public freedom when, beyond making money, something of "real value" — something socially or ecologically meaningful — is being created (Noll 2012: 289).

企业主要通过创造利润获得社会责任——这不仅为企业带来优势，也为各利益相关者和社会提供额外利益。然而，只有当道德上负责任的企业在赚钱之外创造某种"真正有价值"的东西——某种社会或生态上有意义的事物时，才能获得这种公众自由（Noll 2012: 289）。

Section 2 · Case Study
第二节 · 案例研究

Corporate Social Responsibility: The Ford Pinto Case

企业社会责任：福特平托案例

The Ford Pinto case is one of the most cited examples of how prioritising short-term cost savings over human welfare leads to catastrophic consequences — for consumers, for brand trust, and ultimately for the company itself.

福特平托案例是优先考虑短期成本节约而非人类福祉如何导致灾难性后果的最常引用案例之一——对消费者、对品牌信任，以及最终对公司本身。

Case Study
案例详情
Ford Pinto: When Cost-Benefit Meets Human Life
福特平托：当成本收益遭遇人类生命
  • Ford Pinto was a small car built until 1980, designed as competition for European and Japanese imports — with a strict target: under 2,000 lbs weight, under $2,000 cost
  • 福特平托是生产至1980年的小型车，旨在与欧洲和日本进口车竞争——严格目标：重量低于2000磅，成本低于2000美元
  • The rear of the vehicle was designed such that in rear-end collisions, the petrol tank was easily damaged — leaking gasoline significantly increased the risk of fires, leading to several deaths
  • 车辆后部设计使得在追尾碰撞中油箱容易损坏——泄漏的汽油显著增加了火灾风险，导致多人死亡
  • Internal documents (the "Ford Pinto Memo") revealed that Ford was aware of this weakness, but did not want to bear the costs of a design change
  • 内部文件（"福特平托备忘录"）显示，福特知晓这一缺陷，但不愿承担设计变更的成本
  • Ford's unofficial position: it would be more cost-effective to accept potential lawsuits after deaths and pay appropriate compensation, rather than fix the design
  • 福特的非官方立场：在死亡发生后接受可能的诉讼并支付适当赔偿，比修复设计更具成本效益
  • The realisation that Ford's business ethics rated human lives below sales revenue led to lawsuits and legal proceedings
  • 意识到福特的商业伦理将人类生命的价值评定低于销售收入，引发了诉讼和法律程序

The Ford Pinto case demonstrates that companies operate not in a legal vacuum but within a moral society. Legality alone — "we broke no laws" — is an insufficient justification when human lives are at stake.

福特平托案例表明，企业不是在法律真空中运作，而是在道德社会中运作。单纯的合法性——"我们没有违法"——当人类生命处于危险时，是一种不充分的正当理由。

Section 3
第三节

Marketing Ethics & Consumer Sovereignty

营销伦理与消费者主权

Companies must take responsibility in all areas of their economic activity — for the consequences of their actions (doing or refraining from doing) and for the motives that led to those actions. When marketing is understood as market-oriented corporate management, marketing ethics covers the same range of topics as general business ethics (Aßländer 2011: 349). It operates at the interface between company and society.

企业必须在其经济活动的所有领域承担责任——对其行为（作为或不作为）的后果以及导致这些行为的动机负责。当营销被理解为以市场为导向的企业管理时，营销伦理涵盖与一般商业伦理相同的话题范围（Aßländer 2011: 349）。它在企业与社会的交界处运作。

The Consumer Sovereignty Ideal
消费者主权的理想
  • Mature and self-determined consumers are able to know and articulate their needs and realise them in the market (Aßländer 2011: 350)
  • 成熟且自主的消费者能够了解、表达其需求并在市场上实现这些需求（Aßländer 2011: 350）
  • Marketing's task: to satisfy customer needs and act as a problem solver
  • 营销的任务：满足客户需求并充当问题解决者
The Critical Reality
批判性现实
  • With the development of marketing tools, companies are increasingly able to influence consumer needs to reach their own economic goals
  • 随着营销工具的发展，企业越来越能够影响消费者需求以实现自身经济目标
  • Critics argue that consumers are virtually defenceless against company influence attempts — consumer behaviour is driven by stimulus-response mechanisms (Kroeber-Riel 1992: 679)
  • 批评者认为，消费者对企业影响尝试几乎无防御能力——消费行为由刺激-反应机制驱动（Kroeber-Riel 1992: 679）

In response, marketing science developed the concept of "consumer-traded marketing" — addressing the inferior position of consumers and developing consumer-oriented marketing tools, including relationship marketing with a focus on customer loyalty and complaint management (Bruhn 2009, 2012). Marketing has further evolved into a "science of sustainability marketing," expanding the economic dimension with ecological and social components (Balderjahn 2004).

作为回应，营销学发展出"以消费者为导向的营销"概念——解决消费者的弱势地位并开发以消费者为导向的营销工具，包括以客户忠诚度和投诉管理为重点的关系营销（Bruhn 2009, 2012）。营销进一步演变为"可持续性营销科学"，将经济维度扩展到生态和社会成分（Balderjahn 2004）。

Section 4
第四节

Price as Quality Indicator: The Transparency Problem

价格作为质量指标：透明度问题

The quality of a product often lies hidden in small details — the price, on the other hand, immediately catches the eye (Felser 2007: 422). Consumers frequently use price as a heuristic quality indicator: they infer assumed quality from the price through an "indicator-guided judgment process" (Kroeber-Riel/Weinberg 1996: 296f.). The price thus summarises unknown product features and enables purchasing decisions even with limited information.

产品的质量往往隐藏在细节之中——而价格却立刻映入眼帘（Felser 2007: 422）。消费者经常将价格用作启发式质量指标：他们通过"指标引导的判断过程"从价格推断出假设的质量（Kroeber-Riel/Weinberg 1996: 296f.）。价格因此概括了未知的产品特征，使消费者即使在信息有限的情况下也能做出购买决策。

Case Study
案例研究
Luxury Brands in Bangladesh: When Price Disconnects from Production
孟加拉国的奢侈品牌：当价格与生产脱节

Not only discount goods, but luxury labels including Armani, Ralph Lauren, and Hugo Boss produce in the low-wage country Bangladesh — often in the exact same factories as their low-cost counterparts, where workers toil under life-threatening conditions. The retail price of a garment has little connection to its production costs (Wall Street Journal, July 1, 2013).

不仅折扣商品，包括阿玛尼、拉夫劳伦和雨果博斯在内的奢侈品牌也在低工资国家孟加拉国生产——往往与其低成本同类产品在完全相同的工厂，工人在危及生命的条件下劳作。服装的零售价格与其生产成本几乎没有关系（《华尔街日报》2013年7月1日）。

  • BOSS jeans: €249 · Sweatshirt: €790 · ESCADA trousers: €599 · ARMANI jeans: €185
  • BOSS牛仔裤：249欧元 · 卫衣：790欧元 · ESCADA长裤：599欧元 · ARMANI牛仔裤：185欧元
  • C&A jeans: €19 · H&M jeans: €19 · Esprit jeans: €39.99
  • C&A牛仔裤：19欧元 · H&M牛仔裤：19欧元 · Esprit牛仔裤：39.99欧元
  • Textile expert estimate: Primark shirt production cost ~€1.20 · Hilfiger ~€3.80 · G-Star ~€4.60
  • 纺织专家估计：Primark衬衫生产成本约1.20欧元 · Hilfiger约3.80欧元 · G-Star约4.60欧元
  • "The main factor determining the price is, in fact, the brand name" — Ralston Fernandez, ZXY Apparel Buying Solutions
  • "决定价格的主要因素实际上是品牌名称" ——Ralston Fernandez，ZXY服装采购解决方案

Four theses about price and market transparency: (1) Prices make products comparable and create market transparency. (2) Brands are seals of quality ensuring consumer orientation. (3) Brands build trust by standing for an attitude, a standard, and quality assurance. (4) The pricing also communicates the quality of the product and the production process — or fails to do so.

关于价格与市场透明度的四个论题：（1）价格使产品具有可比性并创造市场透明度。（2）品牌是质量标志，确保消费者导向。（3）品牌通过代表一种态度、标准和质量保证建立信任。（4）定价也传达了产品质量和生产过程的信息——或者未能做到这一点。

Section 5
第五节

The Dilemma of Brand Management

品牌管理的困境

Brands want to command a price premium in the market. But if a large part of the product price is based on the brand rather than its assumed quality, this has ethical implications. Price becomes decoupled from quality and can no longer offer consumers orientation about product quality — market comparability and transparency are threatened.

品牌希望在市场上获得溢价。但如果产品价格的很大一部分基于品牌而非其假设的质量，这就具有伦理含义。价格与质量脱钩，无法再为消费者提供关于产品质量的导向信息——市场可比性和透明度受到威胁。

Brand management and brand design face a classic goal conflict: brands must simultaneously serve a price premium market position for companies AND provide market transparency for consumers. These two goals are — especially in the fashion industry — not in conflict but in direct opposition.

品牌管理和品牌设计面临经典的目标冲突：品牌必须同时为企业服务于溢价市场地位，并为消费者提供市场透明度。这两个目标——尤其在时尚行业——不仅存在冲突，而且处于直接对立之中。

Option A: Price Premium
选项A：价格溢价
  • Brands establish a price premium not fully justified by product quality
  • 品牌建立不完全由产品质量支撑的价格溢价
  • Economic function of brands is served, investments in the brand are justified
  • 品牌的经济功能得到满足，品牌投资得到证明
  • But: consumers are misled about the relationship between price and production reality
  • 但：消费者对价格与生产现实之间的关系产生误解
Option B: Market Transparency
选项B：市场透明度
  • Brands ensure market transparency and offer consumers orientation in a complex environment
  • 品牌确保市场透明度，为消费者在复杂环境中提供导向
  • Builds long-term brand loyalty through trust
  • 通过信任建立长期品牌忠诚度
  • But: limits the ability to charge premium prices beyond genuine product quality
  • 但：限制了超出真实产品质量收取溢价的能力

"The consumer should know that we know that he knows that we want to sell him something."

Alex Bogusky, Crispin, Porter & Bogusky

Public criticism of companies often centres on marketing — and shows that the marketing industry must engage with ethical and moral questions. In the age of the internet and social networks, the world becomes more transparent, and consumers have more options than ever to exchange information publicly and far-reachingly. This increases not only the challenges for brand communication, but also the communicative responsibility of consumers themselves — as co-communicators actively involved in building brand images (Aßländer 2011: 356).

公众对企业的批评往往集中在营销上——这表明营销行业必须认真对待伦理和道德问题。在互联网和社交网络时代，世界变得更加透明，消费者比以往任何时候都有更多选择，可以公开且广泛地交流信息。这不仅增加了品牌传播的挑战，也增加了消费者作为共同传播者主动参与品牌形象建设的传播责任（Aßländer 2011: 356）。

Section 6 · Case Study
第六节 · 案例研究

The Brand Is the Core of the Product: Red Bull

品牌是产品的核心：红牛

Red Bull was the birth of a completely new product category — because marketing was the driving force behind the business from the very beginning. Red Bull demonstrates how a brand can become the actual core of the product, and the ethical questions this raises.

红牛是全新产品类别诞生的标志——因为营销从一开始就是这项业务的驱动力。红牛展示了品牌如何成为产品的真正核心，以及这引发的伦理问题。

Target Audience
目标受众
Work Hard. Play Hard.
努力工作，尽情玩乐
People who want clear and focused mental performance, physical performance, who are dynamic and success-oriented — and simultaneously aspire to an active and positive lifestyle.
希望获得清晰专注的心理表现、体能表现、充满活力和以成功为导向的人——同时向往积极正面的生活方式。
Brand Promise
品牌承诺
Gives You Wings
给你插上翅膀
Red Bull is not just another soft drink — it is your liquid boost of energy. New energy for everyone who is mentally and physically active and enjoys having a life. The brand communicates this through Flugtag, Stratos, X-Fighters, motocross sponsorship.
红牛不只是另一种软饮料——它是你的液态能量补充。为所有精神和体力活跃、享受生活的人提供新能量。品牌通过飞行日、平流层、X战士、摩托车越野赞助传达这一理念。
The Ethical Question
伦理问题
Where Does Responsibility End?
责任在哪里结束？
Red Bull's spectacular brand staging comes at a price — considerable dangers are involved. Where are the limits between Red Bull's corporate responsibility and the individual responsibility of extreme athletes and consumers?
红牛壮观的品牌塑造是有代价的——涉及相当大的危险。红牛的企业责任与极限运动员和消费者的个人责任之间的界限在哪里？

The Red Bull case raises a fundamental question: When a brand's identity is built on extremity and risk-taking, where does corporate responsibility for the consequences end — and individual responsibility begin?

红牛案例提出了一个根本性问题：当品牌身份建立在极端主义和冒险精神之上时，企业对后果的责任在哪里结束——个人责任又从哪里开始？
